Know Your Voting Rights

When you go to vote, it's important you know your rights.

  • If you are in line to vote before 8 PM, you have the right to vote.
  • If someone tells you that you are inelligible to vote, ask for a provisional ballot.
  • If you need assistance, you have the right to request this from a poll worker. They can read you the ballot or assist you in understanding how to use the machine.
  • No one can have or wear any candidate material within 100 feet of a polling location.
  • Poll workers are not allowed to talk to voters about who to vote for, political parties, etc.
  • Voters with disabilities or an inability to stand for a prolonged duration are allowed to bypass the voting line.
